GARLIC FRIED RICE & GLAZED STEAK BITES

Jump to Recipe

Prep Time 15 minutes

Cook Time 15 minutes

Total Time 30 minutes

Yields 3

This garlic fried rice with glazed steak bites is incredibly savory, buttery, and full of flavor. the steak is perfectly seared, carmelized on the outside, and so juicy and tender on the inside. And the best part? It's a quick 30-minute dinner that tastes like you spend way longer in the kitchen.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 pound tenderloin, cut into bite-sided cubes
  • 1 tablespoon neutral oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda

SAUCE

  • 2 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons beef broth
  • 1/2 tablespoon light brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon cracked black pepper
  • cornstarch

GARLIC FRIED RICE

  • 2 cups cooked jasmine rice
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 teaspoons minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon gochujang
  • Salt
  • 3 eggs
  • Green onion (sliced for garnish)
  • Black Pepper

INSTRUCTIONS

GARLIC FRIED RICE

  1. In a small bowl, whisk the eggs together with a pinch of salt and black pepper. Set aside.
  2. Heat a wok over medium heat. Add half of the butter and let it melt
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add the cooked jasmine rice and break it up, tossing to coat in the garlic butter.
  5. Add the soy sauce and gochujang. Toss until evenly combined.
  6. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  7. Push the rice outward to create an empty space in the center of the wok. Add the remaining butter to the center.
  8. Pour the whisked eggs into the center and let them set slightly, then gently scramble.
  9. Once softly scrambled, mix the eggs into the rice.
  10. Toss everything together for another 1 to 2 minutes until heated through.
  11. Remove the fried rice from the wok and set aside.

GLAZED STEAK BITES

  1. In a bowl, toss the cubed tenderloin with oil, paprika, salt, black pepper, and baking soda. Let sit for 10 minutes.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together soy sauce, beef broth, brown sugar, cornstarch, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and black pepper.
  3. Heat the wok over high heat until very hot. Add a light coating of neutral oil.
  4. Add the steak in a single layer and sear without moving for 1 to 2 minutes.
  5. Toss and continue cooking until browned on all sides and cooked to your preferred doneness, about 5 to 7 minutes total.
  6. Reduce heat slightly and pour in the sauce. Toss continuously as it thickens and coats the steak, about 1 to 2 minutes.
  7. Remove from heat once glossy and sticky.

ASSEMBLE

  1. Spoon the garlic fried rice into a large serving bowl or divide onto plates.
  2. Add the glazed steak bites right in the center.
  3. Garnish generously with sliced green onions.
  4. Enjoy warm.
